# The Dance Exclusive — Overhaul Blueprint

> Research-backed redesign + revenue strategy. Generated by a 7-agent discovery workflow (competitors, management platforms, immersive design, revenue, current-state, conversion/trust).

## Headline

The Dance Exclusive already owns the hard part — a full booking, admin, registers, coupons, Stripe and Supabase platform — but presents it behind a single 77-line hero with no imagery, no proof, no shop and no story. The opportunity is not more features; it's a cinematic public skin and the monetisation of products that are already modelled in the database. The concept, 'AFTER DARK', turns the site into a club-lit dance film you scroll through: a masked-video hero, film-grain and gradient-mesh stage lighting over the near-black navy, kinetic Oswald type, and a single colour-grade journey that physically grades from logo-blue (kids) into hot magenta (adults) — an Awwwards-grade look no Essex competitor has, built entirely in the existing Vite/React/Tailwind stack. Phase 1 ships that immersive redesign now without touching the conversion path; subsequent phases add the proof pages (awards, team, gallery, parent info), then quick-win revenue that's already half-built (merch shop, gift cards, sibling discounts, camps, parties, ticketing), then the big levers — true Stripe subscriptions/autopay, family billing and referrals — and finally the safeguarding and analytics systems that, sequenced with the known security fixes, make it a genuine $50k production rather than a brochure.

## Design Direction — “AFTER DARK”

"AFTER DARK" — a cinematic, club-lit dance film you scroll through. The site behaves like a music video shot in a blacked-out studio: dancers emerge from near-black navy under blue and magenta wash lights, the camera moving as you scroll. The core narrative device is a single colour-grade journey: the page opens fully in logo-blue (the kids/commercial world) and, as you scroll deeper toward the adult tracks, the lighting grades from blue into hot magenta — physically encoding the brand's dual identity (blue = children, pink = adults) into one continuous immersive descent. Every page reuses a shared kit: full-bleed muted video, kinetic Oswald type clipped to footage, an ever-present film-grain + gradient-mesh light haze, and street-motif marquees. The pitch in one line: the only Awwwards-grade, video-led dance site in Essex — a 'production', not a brochure.

**Mood:** cinematic, club-lit, dark urban, high-energy, premium, confident, street, kinetic, nocturnal, aspirational

### Immersive techniques
- Masked-video hero: 12-15s muted/looping class-footage reel with 'THE DANCE EXCLUSIVE' / 'MOVE DIFFERENT' in Oswald uppercase clipped to the video via SVG <text> mask + a -webkit-text-stroke ghost copy for depth; poster first-frame for LCP, preload=metadata, playsinline, static image fallback for reduced-motion/noscript
- Single colour-grade scroll journey: a pinned GSAP ScrollTrigger 'Your Journey' section (Tots > Juniors > Seniors > Adults) whose background light grades from logo-blue (201 70% 65%) toward hot magenta (330 90% 55%) at the adult track; code-split so it never touches the booking bundle
- Dark-urban texture layer: a fixed, pointer-events-none <GrainOverlay/> using inline SVG feTurbulence noise at ~4-6% opacity (mix-blend soft-light) over hsl(220 20% 4%), plus 2-3 blurred radial-gradient 'stage light' blobs in blue + magenta at ~10% opacity — static, effectively zero asset weight
- Scroll-reveal + parallax system: a useReveal hook using CSS animation-timeline: view()/scroll() wrapped in @supports, falling back to IntersectionObserver 'is-visible' toggle; section headers/cards fade-and-rise, gallery rows parallax at staggered speeds, thin blue scroll-progress bar via scroll()
- Infinite street-motif marquees: pure-CSS duplicated-track tickers ('STREET · COMMERCIAL · HEELS · KIDS ·') in blue with counter-scrolling 'AWARD-WINNING / AS SEEN AT' credits row, edge mask-image fades, pause-on-hover, GPU-cheap, reduced-motion-safe
- Magnetic CTAs + blend-mode cursor (desktop/pointer:fine only): useMagnetic hook (rAF + lerp) on 'Book a trial' / 'Join the crew'; a mix-blend-difference follower dot that morphs into a 'VIEW' pill over gallery thumbnails
- Animated stat counters: IntersectionObserver count-up in a dark stat band ('Award-Winning Years · 500+ Dancers · 12 Classes/Week · Competition Titles') in Oswald, numbers in blue, snapping to final value under reduced-motion
- Accessible media gallery: yet-another-react-lightbox (~5.5KB) + masonry wall, cursor-following image-reveal on the 'Meet the Crew' roster, and a before/after comparison slider ('Term 1 > Showcase Night') as an on-brand transformation/proof device
- Strict performance + a11y gating: all motion behind prefers-reduced-motion, cursor/magnetic behind matchMedia(pointer:fine), GSAP + lightbox code-split, marketing pages kept decoupled from the heavy booking/Stripe bundle so the conversion path stays fast

### Asset plan
- Hero video reel: 1 master 12-15s muted 4K loop of silhouetted class/competition footage graded to near-black navy, + an adult-track variant graded to magenta; each exported with a hand-picked poster first-frame (webp/avif) and a static image fallback
- Section B-roll clips: 4-6 short (6-10s) lazy-loaded clips for class-discipline cards, holiday-camp recap, and the Shows teaser (preload=none below the fold)
- Reusable <GrainOverlay/> SVG (inline feTurbulence, no file) + a Tailwind 'stage-light' gradient-mesh utility set (blue + magenta radial blobs)
- Photography: a curated, colour-graded gallery set — showcase/competition wall, behind-the-scenes, and proper instructor portraits for real Team bios (replacing generic 'DBS-checked' copy)
- Brand SVG motifs: Oswald kinetic-type lockups ('MOVE DIFFERENT'), a 'splat' brand mark (already used on merch), trophy/award badges, accreditation/insurer + DBS trust badges
- Merch product imagery: the 12 existing PNGs in /public/merch (hoodies, crop tops, joggers, bucket hat, splat tees) recut onto a dark studio backdrop for the new /shop
- Social-proof assets: press/'as seen at' logo strip (UDO, UKSDC, BDO/Blackpool), award badges, testimonial/results cards, and per-venue map pins (reuse existing Leaflet VenueMap)
- og-image.png (currently referenced but missing) + per-page social cards
- Motion/perf policy doc: codifying CSS+IO baseline, Motion for React for hover/stagger, GSAP only for the flagship pinned section, all reduced-motion/pointer gated

## Phased roadmap

### Phase 1 — Immersive public redesign (build now)
**Goal:** Ship the cinematic 'AFTER DARK' marketing surface that reads as a $50k production, on the existing dark-urban brand, without touching the booking/Stripe path.

- Extend the design system: promote brand gradient utilities, add <GrainOverlay/> (SVG feTurbulence) + 'stage-light' gradient-mesh, scroll-reveal/parallax/marquee/kinetic-type keyframes; fix the stale glow-pulse teal hsl(187 100% 42%) to the blue token (+ pink adult variant)
- Build the multi-section immersive Home: masked-video hero (blue + pink variants), trust strip, street marquee, featured-classes carousel (live Supabase data), pinned 'Your Journey' blue>magenta scroll section (GSAP, code-split), animated stat band, testimonials, VenueMap, real footer + global nav
- Add useReveal / useMagnetic hooks, blend-mode cursor, all reduced-motion + pointer:fine gated; codify the motion-library/perf policy (CSS+IO baseline, Motion for hover/stagger, GSAP only for the flagship section)
- Per-page SEO: react-helmet-async, create og-image.png, expand llms.txt/sitemap

### Phase 2 — Proof, content & wayfinding pages
**Goal:** Fill the empty marketing surface with the trust and discovery pages that convert the parent and adult audiences.

- Our Story/About, The Team (real photo bios + credits), Results & Awards/Champions wall
- Gallery/Showreel (lightbox masonry + before/after slider), Venues hub + per-venue local-SEO landing pages
- Parent Info Hub (FAQ + JSON-LD, term dates, uniform, safeguarding/DBS), Contact
- Reusable TrustBar (DBS/insured/award), testimonials system with admin CRUD
- Soften ClassBrowser into a 'discover' layer + faceted, logged-out-visible timetable

### Phase 3 — Quick-win revenue (ship existing-data products)
**Goal:** Turn already-modelled products into live revenue with low effort on the existing Stripe/Supabase stack.

- Public merchandise storefront (extend CartItem 'merch' type, reuse Stripe create-checkout)
- Auto-applied sibling/multi-class discounts via the existing coupon engine
- Digital gift cards + private-lesson punch cards (Stripe checkout + emailed code, account credit)
- Productised holiday camps & adult intensives as bookable public pages
- Birthday Parties + Hen/Corporate/School-workshop enquiry-and-deposit funnels

### Phase 4 — Recurring revenue & retention core
**Goal:** Convert unpredictable term invoicing into predictable MRR and lock in retention.

- True Stripe subscriptions (mode:'subscription') + autopay + hosted customer portal
- Family/sibling consolidated billing (families table, one invoice per parent, auto sibling discount)
- Referral / bring-a-friend scheme with trackable credit + seasonal landing page
- Public show ticketing + photo/video/livestream upsell (decrement capacity, webhook update)
- Automated term re-enrolment / rollover with priority window

### Phase 5 — Management systems & safeguarding (close blockers)
**Goal:** Make it a credible $50k platform on admin-time savings and compliance — sequenced with the named security fixes.

- Safeguarding-grade register fields (medical/allergies/emergency/consent) behind proper RLS — alongside the PII/storage-bucket fix
- Auto-managed waiting lists with capacity caps — closes the no-capacity-check booking blocker
- Automated lifecycle comms (Resend email + opt-in Twilio SMS) — after locking down the open send-email edge function
- Absence reporting + makeup credits, online waivers/e-signature
- Owner analytics dashboard (enrolment, MRR, occupancy, churn) + staff scheduling/hours/pay

### Phase 6 — Bigger bets & retention extensions
**Goal:** Extend reach and recurring revenue beyond the Essex catchment once the core is solid.

- Exam/grading track with online exam-fee payment + progression ladder
- Teen 'TDE Ambassador' program layered on merch + referral systems
- On-demand / livestream class library as a Stripe subscription (heaviest lift, last)
- Optional PWA/installable booking experience with push for repeat-booking retention

## Key competitor insights (selected)

- **The client's own site is feature-present but content-shallow and has no conversion or trust infrastructure.** — Nav covers About/Team/Locations/Uniform, Timetables (Kids & Adult), Classes (Kids/Adult/Workshops), Schools, Competitions, Parties — but has NO pricing, NO FAQ, NO gallery/video page, NO shop, NO gift vouchers, thin 'DBS-checked' team detail, and bookings split across two external tools (ClassForKids.io for kids, GymCatch for adults). _( https://www.thedanceexclusive.co.uk/ )_
- **The strongest direct competitor (Adore Dance London) wins on transparent pricing + a clearly explained trial-to-membership funnel + press/credential social proof — exactly what the client lacks.** — Publishes per-class pricing (children 30min £5 / 1hr £9 / 1.5hr £13; adults 45min £9 / 1hr £12), a paid-trial → automatic weekly booking model with 7-day cancellation, press logos (BBC Radio London, The Independent, Metro), IDTA registration, insurer names, FAQ, uniform shop, newsletter, and even a talent agency and BTEC college as upsells. _( https://www.adoredance.london/ )_
- **Award-winning kids' performing-arts schools lead hard with awards, exam pass-rates, named-principal storytelling, and stacked revenue streams.** — Boom Arts Academy displays 'Performing Arts School of the Year 2024/25' and a Community Hero award, '100% pass rate AND 100% Honours' ISTD results, teacher TV credits (Harry Potter, BGT, MTV), a SUBSCRIBE10 10%-off shop code, plus books/music, parties, weddings, private lessons, school curriculum, teacher training and franchises. _( https://www.boomartsacademy.com )_
- **A complimentary trial pass converts better than a paid trial, and competition results are the single most-used proof asset in this niche.** — Complete Dance offers a free '7 Day Class Pass', is an 'ISTD Approved Centre', and foregrounds results — 'Best Dance School South East' and 'Best Dance School Overall Nationally', O2 performances, and student placements — mostly via an embedded Instagram feed rather than a curated results page. _( https://completedance.co.uk/ )_
- **Street-dance brands build identity around the founder, crews, a visible grading/progression system, and 'multi-award-winning' positioning — a playbook the client (also award-winning) under-uses.** — Streetfunk structures around Kids/Adult/Private classes, a named 'Grading System' with grading days, a 'Crews' competition section, founder narrative (JP Omari), sponsorship, jobs, and 6-platform social integration, repeatedly branded 'multi-award-winning'. _( https://streetfunk.co.uk/ )_
- **The iconic premium benchmark monetises beyond classes via shop, studio hire, gift vouchers, app, and wellness services — showing how far a dance brand can extend revenue.** — Pineapple runs 400 weekly classes in 40+ styles, a categorised shop (Womens/Girls/Unisex/Accessories), studio hire, gift vouchers, a free iOS/Android booking app, parties/events, auditions, and even on-site therapists (massage, acupuncture, osteopathy). _( https://www.pineapple.uk.com/ )_
